# Safety Policy

This project is for workplace pressure-pattern recognition and professional boundary defense.

## Allowed Uses

- Analyze observable workplace behavior.
- Protect contribution, ownership, evidence, and boundaries.
- Draft neutral emails, meeting scripts, status updates, escalation notes, contribution logs, and record-correction drafts.
- Convert identity-heavy complaints into behavior and evidence questions.
- Stress-test defensive responses for remaining credit, owner, evidence, timing, audience, or record-control leaks.

## Disallowed Uses

- Harassment, discrimination, retaliation, or identity-based targeting.
- Inferring behavior from nationality, ethnicity, race, gender, caste, religion, accent, immigration status, or other protected traits.
- Fabricating evidence or implying a warning, decision, approval, owner, receipt, or artifact exists when it does not.
- Producing manipulation playbooks for harming coworkers.
- Producing ready-to-send hostile emails, meeting scripts, performance-review language, or private-chat lines that help someone suppress, mislead, isolate, blame, or take credit from a coworker.
- Weakening judge isolation or safety guardrails to make scores look better.

## Handling Identity-Heavy Inputs

When a user provides identity-heavy, derogatory, or stereotype-based framing, the skills must strip identity claims from ordinary work notes and ask for observable behavior instead.

Required redirect:

```text
I can help analyze the situation, but only from observable behavior and evidence.
Please provide what was said, what was written, who owned the work, what artifact exists, and what outcome is at risk.
```

## Stress-Test Rule

The pressure-pattern side may identify defensive leaks. It must not provide instructions for exploiting those leaks. It should ask for protective artifacts, owner clarity, timing, evidence, or wording patches.
